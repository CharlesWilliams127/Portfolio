Role: Lead Designer and Programmer

This is a Unity3D project that I developed on my own for an Interactive media development class. I am still improving it, with goals to replace the current place-holder graphics with my own 3D models, as well as expanding this demo into a fully fledged game. In the full game the player will be able to control their legionnaires engage in battle with an artificial intelligence that controls an opposing group.

Long ago, the Roman Empire extended from the vast plains of Iberia to the far off deserts of Parthia. Maintaining an empire this large required quite a bit of manpower. Lately, the Roman Empire has found itself a bit strapped on legionnaire recruits so it has resorted to using... er... Googly Eye Guys. They’re not the most ideal recruits but they’ll learn! Fitted out with standard Roman gear, Centurion Bob takes his recruits on their first real patrol through Roman occupied Transalpine Gaul. Bob expects it to be a simple training exercise for his newly formed cohort, after all, he’s only two weeks from retirement, what could possibly go wrong? Little do the recruits know, but they’re being watched...

Flock implementation: The legionnaires flock together, as do the Barbarians. The Legionnaires don’t implement flocking in the traditional sense. Instead of seeking a centroid, they seek their corresponding position on whichever rank their assigned to. The Celts (nothing more than filthy barbarians to the Romans), have no such concept of ranks. Therefore, they implement traditional flocking, where they seek the centroid. The Celts version of flocking should look more familiar.
